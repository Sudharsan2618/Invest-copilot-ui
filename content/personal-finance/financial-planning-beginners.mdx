"Financial planning? That's for people with money." If you've ever thought this, you're exactly the person who needs financial planning the most.

Financial planning isn't about being rich. It's about making sure **today's choices don't destroy tomorrow's options.** It's the difference between a 40-year-old who's comfortable and a 40-year-old who's panicking.

And it's simpler than you think.

<Analogy>
  Financial planning is like using Google Maps for a long road trip. You CAN
  drive without maps — but you'll take wrong turns, waste fuel, hit dead ends,
  and maybe never arrive. With Maps, you see the route, estimated time, traffic,
  and alternate routes. You still drive — but you know where you're going.
  That's all a financial plan is: your money's GPS.
</Analogy>

**The monthly SIP column is the key.** Notice how most goals need ₹8,000-15,000/month — not lakhs per month. Starting early makes impossible-sounding goals achievable with modest monthly amounts.

<IndianContext>
  The average Indian wedding costs ₹12-25 lakhs (major cities). A decent 2BHK in
  a tier-1 city costs ₹60L-1.2Cr (you need 20% down payment). Engineering
  college fees are ₹8-15L for 4 years. IIM MBA is ₹20-25L. These numbers will be
  2-3x higher by the time your children need them due to education inflation
  (10-15% per year).
</IndianContext>

<KeyConcept title="The Power of Starting at 25 vs 30">
  Starting just 5 years earlier can nearly DOUBLE your final corpus. A ₹10,000/month SIP at 12% returns:
  - **Starting at 25 (30 years):** ₹3.53 crores
  - **Starting at 30 (25 years):** ₹1.90 crores

Those 5 years of delay cost you **₹1.63 crores.** That's the most expensive procrastination of your life.

</KeyConcept>

<CaseStudy title="Lakshmi's Simple Plan That Changed Everything">
  **Lakshmi**, 24, a teacher in Coimbatore earning ₹28,000/month, felt she earned "too little" for financial planning. Here's the simple plan she made in 15 minutes:

**Goal 1: Emergency Fund** — ₹1,00,000 in 12 months → ₹8,500/month in savings account
**Goal 2: Upskilling Course** — ₹50,000 in 18 months → ₹2,700/month in RD
**Goal 3: Long-term Wealth** — Start small → ₹2,000/month SIP in Nifty 50 index fund

**Total monthly commitment: ₹13,200** (47% of income — aggressive but she shared housing with family)

**After 2 years:** Emergency fund complete (₹1,05,000), course completed (salary increased to ₹35,000), SIP portfolio worth ₹52,000. Total savings/investments: ₹1,57,000 — from a ₹28,000 salary.

She didn't need a financial advisor. She needed 15 minutes, a calculator, and the decision to start.

</CaseStudy>
