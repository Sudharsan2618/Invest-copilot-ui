Here's a bold claim: **you're already tracking your expenses.** You just don't know it.

If you use UPI (and 95% of urban Indians do), every single transaction is already recorded â€” with date, time, amount, and who you paid. Your Google Pay or PhonePe history IS your expense tracker. You've been making a financial diary every day without realizing it.

The problem was never "how do I track?" The problem is: **"how do I look at the data I already have?"**

<Analogy>
  Tracking expenses is like stepping on a weighing scale. The scale doesn't make
  you gain or lose weight â€” it just tells you where you are. Some people avoid
  the scale because they're afraid of the number. Similarly, many people avoid
  looking at their spending because they're afraid of what they'll find. But you
  can't lose weight without weighing yourself, and you can't fix spending
  without seeing it clearly.
</Analogy>

## The 10-Minute Sunday Review

Forget daily tracking. Forget apps that send 20 notifications a day. Here's the only system you need:

**Every Sunday evening, spend 10 minutes doing this:**

1. Open Google Pay / PhonePe â†’ Transaction History
2. Scroll through the week's transactions
3. Mentally categorize: **Need it** or **Wanted it**
4. Note any surprises (expenses you forgot about)
5. Write ONE number: **Total spent this week**

That's it. No spreadsheet. No app. Just 10 minutes of awareness.

<KeyConcept title="Why Sunday Reviews Work Better Than Daily Tracking">
  Daily tracking feels like homework â€” you'll quit in 2 weeks. A weekly review
  shows you **patterns**: "Oh, I always overspend on Fridays." "I ordered food 4
  times this week, not the 2 I thought." Patterns are more actionable than
  individual transactions.
</KeyConcept>
