## So What IS the Stock Market?

The stock market is simply the **organised marketplace** where these ownership pieces are bought and sold. It has three key roles:

### Role 1: Helping Companies Raise Money (Primary Market)

When a company needs crores to expand, it can sell shares to the public through an <JargonTooltip term="IPO" definition="Initial Public Offering â€” when a company sells shares to the public for the first time">IPO</JargonTooltip>. This is the **primary market** â€” where new shares are created and sold for the first time.

<IndianContext>
  India saw some massive IPOs recently: Zomato raised â‚¹9,375 crores (2021), LIC
  raised â‚¹21,000 crores (2022), and Hyundai India raised â‚¹27,870 crores (2024) â€”
  the largest Indian IPO ever. When you invest in an IPO, your money goes
  directly to the company for growth. It's like funding Meera's chai business,
  but at scale.
</IndianContext>

### Role 2: Letting Investors Trade Shares (Secondary Market)

After the IPO, shares trade between investors on the **secondary market** â€” this is what people usually mean when they say "stock market." Here, the money doesn't go to the company â€” it goes between buyers and sellers.

Think of it like buying a flat. The builder gets your money when you buy a new flat (primary market). But when you sell that flat to someone else 5 years later, the builder doesn't get anything â€” the money goes between you and the buyer (secondary market).

### Role 3: Discovering Price (What's a Company REALLY Worth?)

Every second, millions of buyers and sellers are voting with their money: "I think TCS is worth â‚¹3,800/share" vs "I think it's only worth â‚¹3,700." This constant negotiation is called <JargonTooltip term="Price Discovery" definition="The process by which buyers and sellers collectively determine the market price of an asset through their buy/sell orders">price discovery</JargonTooltip>.

<KeyConcept title="The Stock Price Is an Opinion, Not a Fact">
  A stock price of â‚¹1,500 doesn't mean the company is "worth" â‚¹1,500 per share
  in some absolute sense. It means that right now, in this moment, the most
  someone is willing to pay is â‚¹1,500, and the least someone is willing to sell
  for is â‚¹1,500. Tomorrow, that opinion could change â€” even if the company
  hasn't changed at all.
</KeyConcept>
