You know what an index is. Now let's understand the vehicle that lets you OWN the entire index in one click.

An index fund is so simple that it's almost anticlimactic: **it just buys everything in the index.** No genius fund manager. No secret strategy. No proprietary algorithm. Just ... copy the list.

And this "boring" approach beats 73% of professional fund managers over 10 years.

<Analogy>
  Imagine you want to eat exactly what Virat Kohli eats. Option A: hire a chef
  to guess what Kohli eats and try to replicate it (active fund). Option B: get
  Kohli's actual meal plan and follow it exactly (index fund). Option B is
  simpler, cheaper, and more accurate. An index fund doesn't try to BEAT the
  market — it IS the market.
</Analogy>

<KeyConcept title="There's No Active Decision-Making">
  An index fund manager doesn't decide "should I buy more Reliance?" or "is HDFC
  Bank overvalued?" They simply follow the index recipe. If Reliance is 10% of
  Nifty, the fund holds 10% in Reliance. If NSE drops Zee from the index and
  adds Divi's Labs, the fund sells Zee and buys Divi's Labs. Zero judgment, zero
  opinion, zero ego.
</KeyConcept>
