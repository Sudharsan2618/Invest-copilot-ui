Imagine you're at a buffet with 50 dishes. You can either:

**A)** Pick just 3 dishes (and hope they're all delicious)
**B)** Take a tiny spoonful of all 50 (guaranteed a great meal overall)

Option A is stock picking. Option B is index investing. Let's see why Option B wins for almost everyone.

<Analogy>
  Think of the Indian cricket team. When India wins, it's rarely because of ONE
  player. It's because the TEAM performs — if Kohli fails, Rohit delivers. If
  bowlers struggle, batsmen score 400. Diversification works the same way: when
  one stock in your portfolio fails, others carry the team. But if your
  portfolio IS just one or two stocks, you're betting on one batsman to win
  every match alone.
</Analogy>

## The Brutal Math of Stock Picking

<KeyConcept title="Most Individual Stocks Underperform">
  A landmark study by Professor Hendrik Bessembinder found that globally, just
  **4% of stocks** generated ALL of the stock market's wealth creation. The
  other 96% collectively matched or underperformed government bonds. In simpler
  terms: out of 100 stocks, about 4 become the Reliances and TCSes, a few dozen
  are mediocre, and the majority either go nowhere or lose money. Picking those
  4 winners out of 100 is extraordinarily difficult.
</KeyConcept>

<IndianContext>
  SEBI's own study (January 2023) revealed that **89% of individual traders in

  too, most retail stock pickers underperform the Nifty 50 benchmark over 3+
  years. The reason isn't stupidity — it's that competing against professional
  institutions with 100x your resources, data, and speed is structurally unfair.
  Index funds eliminate this unfair competition entirely.

<CaseStudy title="Two Friends: ₹6 Lakhs Each, Very Different Outcomes">
  **Arun** was confident. He put ₹6L in 3 stocks he was "sure about" in 2018: ₹2L each in Yes Bank ("great banking franchise"), Vodafone-Idea ("telecom is essential"), and DHFL ("housing will always grow").

**Rahul** quietly put ₹6L in a Nifty 50 index fund.

**By 2024:**

- Arun's portfolio: Yes Bank ₹2L → ₹3,000. Vodafone-Idea ₹2L → ₹22,000. DHFL ₹2L → ₹0 (delisted). **Total: ₹25,000.** A 96% loss.
- Rahul's index fund: ₹6L → **₹14,00,000** (at ~15% CAGR from 2018 lows to 2024 highs).

**Same starting money. Same time period.** Arun has ₹25,000. Rahul has ₹14 lakhs. The difference = diversification.

And here's the kicker: Arun's stocks were ALL recommended by "experts" at the time. They were in popular portfolios, had analyst buy ratings, and seemed like "safe" picks. **You can't know in advance which companies will fail.**

</CaseStudy>
