<KeyConcept title="Why Active Managers Struggle in Large-Cap">
  India's large-cap space (top 100 companies) is heavily researched â€” hundreds
  of analysts covering the same stocks. There's very little "hidden"
  information. When everyone knows everything, it's nearly impossible for any
  one manager to consistently know MORE than the market. This is called market
  efficiency. In contrast, mid-cap and small-cap stocks have fewer analysts â†’
  more opportunity for skilled managers to find hidden gems. That's why active
  management does relatively better in smaller stocks.
</KeyConcept>

<IndianContext>
  SEBI's 2018 recategorization forced large-cap funds to invest 80%+ in the top
  100 companies. This made them essentially closet index funds â€” they hold
  mostly the same stocks as the index but charge 5-10x higher fees. Before 2018,
  "large-cap funds" could secretly load up on mid-caps to boost returns. Now
  that they can't, their inability to beat Nifty 50 is even more visible.
</IndianContext>
