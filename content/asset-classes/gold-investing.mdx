Your grandmother had gold. Your mother has gold. And now everyone from YouTube to your colleague tells you to "buy gold for safety."

Gold has been India's favourite asset for 5,000 years. But most Indians buy gold the WORST possible way — and leave crores on the table doing so. Let's fix that.

<Analogy>
  Gold is like a raincoat. You don't wear it on sunny days (bull markets). It
  doesn't make you look great. But when the storm hits (market crash,
  geopolitical crisis, currency collapse), everyone who has one is grateful.
  Gold isn't meant to make you rich — it's meant to protect you when everything
  else is falling apart.
</Analogy>

<CaseStudy title="The Real Cost of Gold Jewellery as 'Investment'">
  **Lakshmi** bought a gold necklace in 2019: 20g at ₹3,500/g = ₹70,000 gold value + 20% making charges (₹14,000) + 3% GST (₹2,520) = **₹86,520 total**.

**Her friend Divya** invested ₹86,520 in Sovereign Gold Bonds the same month.

**In 2024 (gold at ₹7,200/g):**

- Lakshmi's necklace: sells at ₹7,200 × 20g = ₹1,44,000 (jeweller deducts 2% for purity testing). **Net: ₹1,41,120.** Profit: ₹54,600. LTCG tax (12.5%): ₹6,825. **After-tax profit: ₹47,775.**
- Divya's SGB: Gold value ₹1,44,000 + 5 years of 2.5% interest on ₹70,000 face value (~₹8,750) = ₹1,52,750. Tax on gold gains at maturity: **₹0 (tax-free)**. **Total profit: ₹66,230.**

**Divya earned ₹18,455 MORE** from the same amount — because she avoided making charges and earned interest. And she didn't have to worry about locker fees or theft.

</CaseStudy>
