## The Most Important Concept in Investing

If the previous articles were about understanding the problem (inflation, loss of purchasing power), this article is about understanding the **solution.** Compounding is how ordinary people become wealthy. Not through lottery wins, inheritance, or stock tips â€” through patience and mathematics.

Albert Einstein (may have) called it the "eighth wonder of the world." Warren Buffett, worth $130 billion, earned 99% of his wealth after age 50 â€” not because he was a genius in his 50s, but because his investments had been compounding for 40+ years by then.

**Compounding is simple to understand. It's hard to be patient enough to benefit from it.**

<Analogy>
Imagine you have a magical mango tree. In Year 1, it produces 10 mangoes. But each mango contains seeds that grow into new trees. By Year 5, you don't just have more mangoes from the original tree â€” you have mangoes from the daughter trees. And those daughter trees are producing their own daughter trees.

By Year 20, you have an entire orchard. Thousands of mangoes per year. All from one seed. The first 5 years feel slow (only 10, 21, 34, 50, 69 mangoes). But by year 20, you're getting 600+ per year.

**Your investments work the same way.** The returns generate their own returns. The "daughter trees" are the interest you earn on your interest.

</Analogy>

### The Snowball Effect

A snowball rolling downhill starts tiny. For the first few meters, it barely grows. But it picks up more snow with each rotation. By the time it's traveled 100 meters, it's enormous â€” not because any single rotation added much, but because EACH rotation added to a progressively larger ball.
